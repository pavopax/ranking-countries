Ranking Countries

We're all familiar with various rankings of the world's universities. Recently, however, US News came out with a ranking of the world's countries. Partly in order to investigate their methodology, this project tried to recreate the US News ranking, to contrast what is shown by propriatary survey data (the basis for the US News ranking) with hard, real metrics that are publicly accessible (which is what this ranking is based on).

Data Details

The ranking from US News had 60 "best" countries. These were selected for my plots in the above links. All data is downloaded live from the World Bank via use of their API.

Other Notes

One of the project aims is to compare and contrast perception (US News survey data) with measured statistics (World Bank, other data), and perform analyses to determine sensitivity of the various measurements. For example, US News seemed to use unadjusted GDP per capita, while adjusting it for purchasing power may be better.

The project will feature customizable rankings via adjustable weighting of the attributes, so that anyone can find their own "best country."

Toolkit

Data for the Data Explorer was pre-processed with Pandas, and the output is a Bokeh plot. Other data munging was done with R, and the plot from Week 1 was done in D3, based on a template from NVD3. Page styling was done with Bootstrap (plus some minor CSS and HTML tweaks - Safari console debugger was very useful!). Everything was packaged together with Flask (forms use Flask-WTF), and deployed on Heroku. Favicon is my own, created on Paper for iOS and converted with favicon.cc
